This experiment starts with a good guess for initial conditions. We see stable oscillation for about 2200 time steps. Then the amplitude of the oscillations increase and the experiment ends with prey extinction.
The reason for falling out of stability is not obvious. It could be to do with that the max of the prey's oscillations between 2000 and 2750 time steps is increasing whereas the predator's is decreasing.

To investigate this, we could look at the difference per oscillation between the predator's max and the prey's max, and the difference in time between these maxes occurring. The change in those numbers may help to explain why the population sizes may suddenly spin out.
